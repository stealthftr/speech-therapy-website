## 1. The emotional + structural spine: "What you're noticing"

Every high-intent parent arrives with a *specific thing they've observed*. The site's
job is to (a) reflect that observation back so they feel understood in seconds, then
(b) route them to the offer. This "noticing → understanding → next step" path is the
spine of the home page and the paid ad landing pages.

**The noticings we build around (speech-only):**

| What the parent notices | What they're afraid of | Our reframe / route |
|---|---|---|
| "She's 2 and barely says a word." | Falling behind, autism, "did I cause this" | Late-talking is the #1 reason to assess early → Assessment |
| "I can't understand my own child." | They'll struggle at school, be teased | Speech clarity is measurable and treatable → Assessment |
| "He gets stuck / repeats sounds." | Stuttering for life | Early fluency support works → Assessment |
| "Meltdowns when he can't be understood." | Behaviour problem | It's a *communication* problem, and it's fixable → Assessment |
| "The kindy teacher mentioned it." | It's real if a pro noticed | You were right to look → book Assessment |
| "He talks but can't hold a conversation / make friends." | Social isolation | Social communication is speech-path scope → Assessment |
| "Reads the words, doesn't get the story." | Falling behind at school | School-age language therapy → Assessment |
| "He's plateaued / lost words." | Regression | Flag promptly → Assessment + GP |

Copy rule: name the *observation* in the parent's own words first; introduce the
clinical term second. Reassurance before education before CTA.

## 1.5 Brand positioning: the boutique (not a clinic mill)

The core differentiator, threaded through every page: **a specialised, personal
speech practice — not a high-volume clinic mill.** Proof points to lean on:

- **One expert who knows your child** — continuity of care, not a rotating cast of
  junior therapists or a new face every term.
- **We come to you** — therapy at your child's school or home, where they're
  relaxed and skills actually generalise; or online. No sterile waiting rooms.
- **We do one thing, exceptionally** — speech pathology only. Depth, not a menu.
- **Founder-led, hand-picked** — the practice is built around a lead clinician's
  standard, not a franchise playbook.

Tonal payoff: this justifies the premium 90-min tier and the paid assessment *and*
makes the human-led "fit call" funnel (below) feel congruent instead of salesy.
"Boutique" is shown, not said — through the copy, the mobile model, and the
one-to-one language, rarely the literal word.

## 2. Front-end offer: a human "fit call" that routes to the right start

**The primary conversion action across the whole site is booking a free call with a
speech pathologist** — internally the *fit call*, publicly the **Discovery Call**
(or "Getting-Started Call"). It suits the boutique model: consultative and human,
not a transactional checkout, and it lets the expert match each family to the right
starting point rather than forcing one path.

**On the call, the expert diagnoses fit and sells one of two starting points:**
- **Path A — Comprehensive Speech Assessment** (paid, high-margin, remote or
  in-person): for families who want the full picture first, need formal evidence for
  GP/school/NDIS, or aren't sure what's going on.
- **Path B — Start therapy directly** (Signature 90 / Essentials 60): for families
  who already know the concern and just want to begin.

So the site sells the *call*; the call sells the *plan*. The Assessment and Sessions
pages educate and build desire, but their CTA is "Book a free Discovery Call," not a
raw purchase button.

**Assessment offer statement (used on the Assessment page):**
> **The Lark Speech Assessment** — finally understand what's going on with your
> child's speech, and get a clear plan. A certified speech pathologist assesses your
> child, then you receive a plain-English written report, a one-on-one feedback call,
> and specific next steps — whether or not those steps include us.

- "whether or not those steps include us" = the integrity line; the antidote to
  "slimy." Use it wherever the assessment appears.
- Report positioned as usable evidence for **GP, school, and NDIS** — real
  standalone value to families.
- Remote-first: "no waiting rooms; children show their real skills at home."
- Timeline is the weapon against AU waitlists: "seen within [X] days, not [X] months."
  [SWAP honest turnaround.]
- **Fit-call design note:** book via Calendly; intake form captures the "what you're
  noticing" dropdown + funding type so the clinician walks in informed and can steer
  Path A vs B in minutes.

## 4. Session tiers — sell the 90 by framing, never by punishing the 60

Names: **Signature Session (90 min)** vs **Essentials Session (60 min)**.

Framing line: *"Signature is how we do our best work. Essentials exists so cost never
locks a family out of speech therapy."*

| | Signature 90 ⭐ | Essentials 60 |
|---|---|---|
| Therapy time | Extended — more practice, real consolidation, less rushed | Standard, focused |
| Progress email after every visit | ✔ Included | — (reviews at term intervals) |
| Travel / mileage | ✔ Included | Billed per km |
| Best for | Kids who need momentum; school-based (parent not in room) | Budget-conscious; simpler goals |
| Price | ~$300 to NDIS/funding | $[X] + travel |

- **Mileage maths is the silent closer.** Show a transparent worked example: once
  per-visit travel is added to the 60, the real gap to the 90 narrows — let the
  parent reach the conclusion. Publish the per-km rate in plain sight.
- **School kicker for the email:** "At school you're not in the room — the
  after-every-visit email keeps you inside your child's therapy." This is the single
  strongest reason the email is worth paying for; feature it on the Schools page.
- Guardrails: give the 90 a *clinical* rationale (fewer transitions, settling time,
  generalisation, documented handover), not just "more minutes." Never describe the
  60 in negative terms. FAQ must handle "isn't 90 minutes too long for a child?"
  (child-led, play-based, breaks; time includes documentation + parent handover).

## 5. Assessment product design (one SKU at launch)

Inclusions: (1) pre-assessment parent questionnaire; (2) assessment with a certified
speech pathologist (remote video default; in-person option in Adelaide),
standardised + informal measures; (3) plain-English written report (GP/school/NDIS
ready); (4) 20–30 min feedback call; (5) written next-steps plan with home
strategies even if no therapy is recommended. Honest disclosure if some cases need
in-person follow-up.

## 6. Schools channel (the economics engine)

One page, two audiences:
- **Parents:** therapy during the school day, no ferrying kids across town, update
  email after every visit.
- **Principals / coordinators:** zero admin, block scheduling, terms-aligned, one
  invoice. Acquisition hook: free staff PD session or an early-years screening day.
- Growth loop: school density lowers travel cost → consider "partner school =
  travel included on every tier." Update emails are forwardable social proof.
