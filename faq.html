Frequently asked questions

Everything parents ask us, answered honestly.

Don't see your question? It's a perfect opener for a free Discovery Call — or email .

Isn't it too early to worry? Everyone says kids catch up.

Some children do catch up on their own — and some don't, and there's no reliable way to tell which from the outside. What we do know is that earlier support tends to mean faster, easier progress, and "wait and see" has a real cost when a child needed a hand months ago.

That's why our first step is a free Discovery Call, not an enrolment pitch. If your child is tracking well, we'll happily tell you so. Checking early is never the mistake.

How does NDIS funding work with Lark?

We work with self-managed and plan-managed NDIS participants. Speech pathology usually sits under Improved Daily Living in a child's plan. On your free call we'll explain exactly how your funding can be used and what to expect.

How long is your waitlist?

Discovery Calls are usually available within a few days. Because assessments can be done online, we can often see your child far sooner than the months-long waits families face elsewhere. If we're ever genuinely full, we'll tell you and point you to trusted colleagues rather than leave you waiting.

Do you really come to us?

Yes — mobile therapy is central to how Lark works. We see children at their school, kindy or home across metropolitan Adelaide, or online. Being in a familiar place helps children relax and helps their new skills carry into everyday life.

What's the difference between the 90-minute and 60-minute sessions?

Our 90-minute Signature sessions are how we do our best work: more time to warm up and consolidate, travel included, and a progress email after every visit. Our 60-minute Essentials sessions are a more budget-conscious option on par with standard providers, with travel billed separately and progress reviewed at term intervals. Most families choose Signature — especially for school visits, where the after-every-visit email keeps you in the loop when you're not in the room. See the pricing page for a side-by-side.

Isn't 90 minutes too long for a child?

Not the way we run it. Sessions are child-led and play-based, with natural breaks, so it never feels like a 90-minute test. The extra time lets your child settle, do the real work, and practise new skills until they stick — and it includes our documentation and your handover, so you get more, not just a longer appointment.

Do I need a referral from a GP?

No — you can book directly with us, starting with the free call. A GP referral is only needed if you want to access Medicare rebates through a care plan. We'll let you know on your call whether that applies to your family.

What happens at the assessment? Will it stress my child out?

To your child, it looks like playing with a friendly adult and some great activities. To us, it's a structured look at their speech, language and communication using standardised and play-based tasks. You're there the whole time, and afterwards you get a plain-English report, a feedback call, and clear next steps. See the assessment page for the full picture.

Can an online assessment really be accurate?

Yes — for most children, online assessment works very well, and many kids actually show their true skills more readily at home than in an unfamiliar clinic. If your child's situation is better suited to an in-person session, we'll tell you honestly and arrange it in Adelaide.

My child already gets speech help at school. Why add private therapy?

School-based public services are valuable but limited — usually focused on classroom goals and often delivered in groups for short blocks. Private therapy is one-on-one, targets your child's specific needs at their pace, and involves you directly. Many families do both, and with your permission we're happy to coordinate so the plans reinforce each other.

What ages do you see?

Children, from the early years through primary school. Paediatric speech is all we do, which means real depth across late talkers, speech clarity, fluency, understanding language, and school-age communication.

What should I do before the free call?

Nothing, honestly — just show up with what you've noticed. If you'd like to feel prepared, jot down a few examples of what your child says (or doesn't), and download our free milestone guide to see which items made you pause. Your child doesn't need to be on the call.
