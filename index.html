Is this your child?

You know your child better than anyone. If any of these sound familiar, trust it.

Parents don't come to us with diagnoses — they come with a feeling and a few examples. Here's what we hear most. Recognise one? That's reason enough for a free chat.

"They're nearly two and barely saying words."

The worry: are they falling behind, and did I miss it?

Late talking is the most common reason families call us — and the one where an early start helps most. Often a few small changes at home make a big difference.

"I understand them, but no one else can."

The worry: they'll be frustrated, teased, or left out at kindy.

Speech clarity is measurable and very treatable. We work out which sounds are tripping them up and build them, playfully.

"They get stuck — repeating sounds, pushing words out."

The worry: is this stuttering, and will it stick?

Some bumpy speech is a normal phase; some needs support — and it's hard to tell from the outside. A quick chat tells you which.

"Big meltdowns when they can't get their point across."

The worry: is this a behaviour problem?

Often it's a communication problem wearing a behaviour costume. Give a child a way to be understood and the meltdowns usually ease.

"The kindy teacher gently raised it."

The worry: it must be real if a professional noticed.

You were right to take it seriously — and right to look now, not "wait and see." Let's find out where things really stand.

"They talk plenty, but can't hold a conversation or make friends."

The worry: they'll struggle socially and at school.

Understanding language, conversation and social communication are core speech-pathology work — and they're teachable skills.

Funding & cost

The money part, without the runaround.

Most Lark families use NDIS funding, and we'll help you understand exactly how your options work before you commit to anything.

NDIS

We work with self-managed and plan-managed NDIS participants. Speech pathology usually sits under Improved Daily Living. [SWAP registration status]

Medicare

Eligible children may access rebates through a GP-managed care plan (up to 5 allied-health sessions a year). We'll explain if it applies. [SWAP]

Questions parents ask us

The answers you're probably looking for.

Isn't it too early to worry? Everyone says kids catch up.

Some children do catch up on their own — and some don't, and it's genuinely hard to tell which from the outside. That's what a Discovery Call is for. There's no downside to checking: if your child is tracking well, we'll happily tell you so, and you can stop lying awake wondering.

How does NDIS funding work with Lark?

We work with self-managed and plan-managed participants, and speech pathology usually falls under Improved Daily Living. We'll walk you through exactly how your plan can be used on your free call.

Do you actually come to us?

Yes — that's central to how Lark works. We deliver therapy at your child's school or home across Adelaide, or online, so your child is comfortable and skills carry into real life.
